<p align="left">While browsing the excellent photography Stack Exchange site and learning lots about <a href="http://photo.stackexchange.com/q/2706/380#2710" target="_blank">bokeh</a>, I came across the <a href="http://www.diyphotography.net/diy_create_your_own_bokeh" target="_blank">awesome idea</a> that led to this shot:</p>  <p align="left"><img alt="DSC_0417" height="469" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/_IKD9WtY5kxU/TSvU1W0xYuI/AAAAAAAABUs/9UsaqkHQWgk/DSC_0417%5B6%5D.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto; display: block; float: none;" title="DSC_0417" width="700" /></p>
<p>The effect here is in the Christmas lights—they’re regular lights—and by adding this lens filter, stuff that’s normally blurry goes crazy:</p>
<p><img alt="DSC_0445" height="450" src="http://lh5.ggpht.com/_IKD9WtY5kxU/TSvU2YtcG6I/AAAAAAAABU0/LWUzu66h5u8/DSC_0445%5B6%5D.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto; display: block; float: none;" title="DSC_0445" width="426" /></p>
<p>With this in place, the image on the left turns into the image on the right (similar focus distance):</p>  <p align="center"><img alt="DSC_0441" height="450" src="http://lh5.ggpht.com/_IKD9WtY5kxU/TSvU3NwN6II/AAAAAAAABU8/GQhtN5YjZ3k/DSC_0441%5B5%5D.jpg" style="margin: 3px; display: inline;" title="DSC_0441" width="301" />&#160;<img alt="DSC_0440" height="450" src="http://lh5.ggpht.com/_IKD9WtY5kxU/TSvU33kOsQI/AAAAAAAABVA/w30yFiowVck/DSC_0440%5B5%5D.jpg" style="margin: 3px; display: inline;" title="DSC_0440" width="327" /></p>  <p align="left">Putting things farther out of focus yields more dramatic results:</p>  <p align="center"><img alt="DSC_0411" height="400" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_IKD9WtY5kxU/TSvU4kUal1I/AAAAAAAABVI/1R_yMJ3dJE0/DSC_0411%5B8%5D.jpg" style="margin: 3px; display: inline;" title="DSC_0411" width="325" /><img alt="DSC_0419" height="400" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_IKD9WtY5kxU/TSvU5f0jh1I/AAAAAAAABVM/-zrMp32RUsk/DSC_0419%5B7%5D.jpg" style="margin: 3px; display: inline;" title="DSC_0419" width="336" /></p>  <p align="left">As I noted above, this was certainly not my idea—this is just my 10-minute take on it.
